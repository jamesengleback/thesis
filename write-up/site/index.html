Overveiw

I’m structuring this thesis as two independent papers, though some background and methods overlap so these will be shared. The appendix details the software developed for this work.

Overlap

Files relevant to both papers

  • Motivation The end goal for both projects is to re-engineer the Cytochrome P450 BM3 to bind to and turnover the herbicide mesotrione. The background information for the engineering context is in
    • Herbicide-Resistance - background on herbicide resistant crops, their value in food supply chains and approaches to their engineering
    • Enzyme Engineering - background on approaches to enzyme engineering, including directed evolution, in-silico design and machine learning-guided design.
    • Cytochrome P450s - background on the class of enzyme being re-engineered here. Overveiws evolutionary history and phylogenetics, biological function, structure, biochemical mechanism of action, experimental techniques and applications in biotech.
    • BM3 - background on phylogenetics, mechanism of action, structure, heterologous expression, engineering case studies.
    • General Methods - mutageneis, expression and purification

Paper 1

Working Title: Virtual Directed Evolution for Cytochrome P450 Engineering

Summary: Simulate directed evoltuion using protein structure prediction, target ligand docking and scoring and a genetic algorithm to generate iteratively better mutants.

  • Introduction - motivates engineering context, outlines aim and approach.
    • Protein Structure Prediction - background on template-based design, relevant approaches.
    • Docking - background on ligand docking applications, algorithms and limitations
    • Genetic Algorithms - as sequence optimizers
  • Methods - vde paramaters & run details, analysis, lab work
  • Results - docking analysis, evolutionary analysis, lab measured activity
  • Discussion - suitability of products for application, efficacy of technique, proposed improvements

Paper 2

Working Title: Screening Fist - Mapping Cytochrome P450 Substrate Specificity to Sequence with Machine Learning

Summary: Train a machine learning model on P450 BM3 mutant:ligand binding screening data, use model for optimal screening design and to re-design BM3 for herbicide binding.

  • Introduction - motivates engineering context, outlines aim and approach.
    • Deep Learning - perceptrons as a base neural unit, deep learning, transformers, evolutionary scale modelling, graph neural networks
    • High Throughput Screening - background on high throughput screening, SBS plate standaerdisation, liquid handling automation, plate readers, applications in biotech, technology readiness level
  • Methods - model architecture, pre-training data, pre-training, lab screening program, training, planning subsequent screens with adaptive learning.
  • Results - model pre-training accuracy, screening results and analysis, model training & accuracy, adaptive learning.
  • Discussion - suitability of products for application, efficacy of technique, proposed improvements

Appendix - Software

  • enz - a python wrapper for protein structure prediction, molecular docking and its analysis.
  • plates - a python utility for automating plate assay analysis
  • echo - a python utility for designing custom instructions for Labcyte Echo liquid handlers
  • cpd - a python utility for selecting diverse sets of screening compounds
  • mxn - a python utility for automated primer design for protein site-directed mutageneis
